# Strategic Allocation Scenario Framework

## Slide 1 — What We Are Doing

- We build a long-horizon strategic allocation framework across major asset classes
- The system produces 5-year and 10-year return views
- The main conference object is the robust 5-year benchmark portfolio
- We then ask: what macro-financial regimes would justify that benchmark’s behavior?

## Slide 2 — Investment Universe

- US Equities
- Euro Area Equities
- Japan Equities
- China Equities
- Emerging Market Equities
- US Treasuries 7-10Y
- Investment Grade Credit
- Gold
- US REITs

## Slide 3 — Core Horizon

- Main strategic horizon: 5 years
- Secondary reference horizon: 10 years
- The active benchmark for interpretation is the robust 5-year portfolio
- This is a strategic allocation framework, not a short-term trading system

## Slide 4 — What The Model Sees

The scenario layer moves a compact macro-financial state:

- inflation
- unemployment
- short rates
- long rates
- dollar strength
- market volatility
- real rates
- credit spreads
- oil

Across:

- US
- Euro Area
- Japan

## Slide 5 — How Scenario Generation Works

- Start from a real month-end market state
- Move a small set of macro-financial variables in plausible directions
- Keep the broader cross-asset context fixed
- Re-evaluate the benchmark under the new state
- Compare baseline vs scenario:
  - expected long-run return
  - concentration
  - full 9-sleeve allocation

## Slide 6 — How We Label Regimes

The regime layer combines:

- internal macro scores:
  - growth
  - inflation
  - stress
  - rates / conditions
- external anchors:
  - NFCI for financial conditions
  - recession overlay for historical context

This produces interpretable labels such as:

- soft landing
- higher-for-longer
- risk-on reflation
- high-stress defensive

## Slide 7 — The Main Questions

The strongest questions for the benchmark are:

- What does a soft-landing regime look like?
- What does a higher-for-longer regime look like?
- What regime makes gold more attractive?
- What regime improves return without making the benchmark much more concentrated?

## Slide 8 — The Three Cases We Show

1. Upside case
- return improves without destabilizing the portfolio

2. Breadth case
- diversification improves, but not for free

3. Adverse case
- tighter conditions weaken the outlook and keep the portfolio more defensive

## Slide 9 — What The Framework Delivers

- a benchmark-level strategic allocation view
- full portfolio responses across all 9 sleeves
- interpretable macro fingerprints behind each scenario
- regime labels that are easy to explain to investors and allocators

## Slide 10 — Final Takeaway

- This framework is designed to connect long-run capital market views to portfolio allocation decisions
- The benchmark is credible enough to interpret
- The scenario layer shows which plausible regimes support stronger, weaker, broader, or more defensive benchmark outcomes
- The end product is an interpretable strategic allocation scenario engine
