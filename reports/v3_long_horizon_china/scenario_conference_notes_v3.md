# Scenario Notes

- The 60m benchmark remains the main interpretation object: its strongest upside case moved the model-implied portfolio return from 0.1203 to 0.1236 without a large jump in concentration.
- The 120m benchmark is clearly more state-sensitive: its strongest upside case moved from 0.0728 to 0.0736, but it stayed much more concentrated than the 60m benchmark.
- Some deconcentration is possible for the 120m ceiling: in the cleanest case, effective N improved from 3.07 to 3.08 without an obvious return collapse.
- The same variables keep reappearing across cases: short_rate_JP, long_rate_JP, unemp_JP, ig_oas, infl_EA.
- This makes the scenario layer look economically interpretable rather than like a pure optimizer artifact.
- China never becomes a material sleeve under the first-pass scenarios.
- The right public framing is not causal macro truth. These are plausibility-regularized, anchor-local diagnostics around the active benchmark objects.
- For the conference narrative, the simplest message is: the 60m benchmark reacts sensibly and the 120m ceiling helps show what extra state-sensitivity looks like when concentration risk is left less constrained.
