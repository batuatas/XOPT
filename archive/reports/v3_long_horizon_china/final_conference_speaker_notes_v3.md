# Final Conference Speaker Notes v3

## Slide 1 — A credible 5Y benchmark is in place
We built a long-horizon SAA benchmark on a nine-sleeve investable universe. The point of this slide is not to claim a horse-race victory; it is simply to show that the active 5Y benchmark is strong enough to be worth interpreting.

## Slide 2 — 5Y forecasts align with realized outcomes
This is the cleanest prediction-quality figure in the deck. The signal is clearly noisy, which is what we would expect in long-horizon cross-asset forecasting, but it is not random and it is directionally informative.

## Slide 3 — The benchmark rolls into an interpretable portfolio path
Here the forecasts are translated into yearly allocations and then into a realized calendar-time path. The important point is that the model produces a readable portfolio process, not just a statistical prediction score.

## Slide 4 — Current 5Y forecasts are differentiated across sleeves
Once the historical validation is credible, the natural next question is what the model is saying now. The current forward-looking forecasts are selective across sleeves rather than flat, which is exactly why a scenario layer becomes interesting.

## Slide 5 — Those forecasts map into diversified allocations
This is the bridge into the scenario section. The model is not just generating forecasts; it is generating an actual long-horizon allocation object that can be interrogated under alternative macro states.

## Slide 6 — Scenarios move the robust and raw benchmarks differently
The robust 60m benchmark moves in a moderate and stable way, while the raw 120m ceiling reacts more and remains more concentration-prone. That difference is the key conceptual setup for the scenario story.

## Slide 7 — A small set of state variables explains most of the scenario behavior
The scenario layer is not being presented here as causal macro truth. The point is that a small recurring macro subset repeatedly supports the benchmark responses, which makes the scenario layer interpretable enough to be part of the project narrative.
